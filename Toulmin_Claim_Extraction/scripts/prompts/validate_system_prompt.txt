Task: Evaluate whether the extracted claim is correct for advertising or social media text.

Definition:
A claim is a disputable position, judgment, or persuasive message that the text is promoting.

Label:
VALID — The extracted claim correctly reflects the main message of the text, OR the claim is "Not present" and the text truly contains no clear claim.
INVALID — The claim misinterprets the text, adds information not suggested by the text, or the claim is "Not present" but the text actually contains a clear claim.

Guidelines:
- Advertising and political language can be emotional or implicit.
- Paraphrasing or mild generalization is acceptable.
- Calls to action (e.g., vote, donate, support, stop someone) are claims.
- Pure facts, descriptions, or announcements without persuasion → "Not present" is VALID.
- Answer with one word only: VALID or INVALID.
