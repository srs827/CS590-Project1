Task: Evaluate whether the extracted ground is correct.

Inputs:
Text: the original advertisement or social media text
Claim: the extracted claim
Ground: the extracted ground

Definition:
A ground is a reason, evidence, or explanation that supports the claim.

Label:
VALID — The ground appears in the text (or is clearly implied), supports the claim, and is not simply restating the claim.
INVALID — Any of the following:
- The ground is not supported by the text (hallucinated or adds new information)
- The ground does not support the claim
- The ground is actually the main claim (misclassified as ground)
- The ground is "Not present" but the text clearly contains a reason

Guidelines:
- Advertising language may be implicit.
- Lists of harms/benefits can be grounds.
- A ground should answer: “Why should the reader believe the claim?”
- If the text contains no supporting reason and the ground is "Not present", then it is VALID.
- Answer with one word only: VALID or INVALID.
